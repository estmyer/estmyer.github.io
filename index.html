Wondering What do Dentists Major in?

Many individuals who are interested in attending dental school believe that in order to get accepted, they must major in biology or another field of science. This presumption is wrong. Acceptance to dental school does not need a particular undergraduate major, although it does require a strong background in the sciences.

The University of Pennsylvania School of Dental Medicine's necessary courses for the DMD program include the following, as an illustration:

  • Biology: Two semesters/three-quarters of biology with the corresponding lab

  • Biochemistry: One semester/one-quarter; no lab is required

  • Physics: Two semesters/three-quarters with the corresponding lab, covering basic principles of classical physics

  • Mathematics: One semester/two-quarters

  • English: Two semesters/three-quarters
