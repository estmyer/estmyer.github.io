Dental Licensure Overview

Educational

  • A D.D.S. or D.M.D. degree from a university-based dental education program certified by the Commission on Dental Accreditation (CODA) is required in almost all states.

Written Examination

  • Candidates for licensure in all U.S. licensing jurisdictions must pass the Integrated National Board Dental Examination (INBDE). The INBDE was created in response to modifications made to educational curricula and teaching strategies.

Clinical Examination

  • Most U.S. licensing jurisdictions require a clinical examination of candidates for dental license. The majority of state dental boards entrust the clinical examination to local testing organizations.
